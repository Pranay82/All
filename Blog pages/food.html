Shahi Tukda

Shahi Tukda is a rich Mughlai dessert. Can be easily prepared with what’s available in the kitchen.

𝐈𝐧𝐠𝐫𝐞𝐝𝐢𝐞𝐧𝐭𝐬

𝐅𝐨𝐫 𝐑𝐚𝐛𝐝𝐢\𝐑𝐚𝐛𝐫𝐢 (𝐒𝐞𝐫𝐯𝐞𝐬 𝟓-𝟔)
2 litres Milk
4 Cardamom cods
¾ tin Milkmaid/Condensed Milk
Kesar (Optional)
A pinch of Salt
7-8 slices of bread
Ghee\oil for frying

For Sugar syrup

¾ Cup Sugar
Water
For garnish
Almonds and Pistachios (Optional)

𝐌𝐞𝐭𝐡𝐨𝐝

To make Rabdi
1. Take a kadhai/pan, pour the milk and let it come to a boil on low flame.
2. Keep stirring.
3. When the milk reduces to ½ its amount, add the milkmaid.
4. Add ground Cardamom Pods/ Cardamom powder.
5. Add a pinch of salt to enhance the sweetness.
6. Continue cooking till it comes to a thick consistency.
7. Add kesar and let it cool.

𝐓𝐨 𝐟𝐫𝐲 𝐛𝐫𝐞𝐚𝐝 𝐬𝐥𝐢𝐜𝐞𝐬

1. Heat oil/ghee in a kadhai.
2. Meanwhile, cut the bread slices into triangular pieces.
3. Deep fry the bread till golden in colour.

𝐓𝐨 𝐦𝐚𝐤𝐞 𝐬𝐮𝐠𝐚𝐫 𝐬𝐲𝐫𝐮𝐩

1. Take sugar in a vessel, add water to cover it and let it come to a boil.
2. Stir until the sugar melts and turn off the heat.

𝐅𝐨𝐫 𝐠𝐚𝐫𝐧𝐢𝐬𝐡

Slice almonds and pistachios at this stage.

𝐇𝐨𝐰 𝐭𝐨 𝐬𝐞𝐫𝐯𝐞

1. Dip the fried bread slices in the sugar syrup and remove immediately.
2. Keep the bread slices in a serving plate.
3. Pour the rabdi above it.
4. Garnish with almonds and pistachios.

Make it for your loved ones and do share this with your friends and family.

Dhokla

Dhokla is a healthy snack/breakfast which can be made instantly.

𝐈𝐧𝐠𝐫𝐞𝐝𝐢𝐞𝐧𝐭𝐬

1 ½ Cup Besan (Sifted)
1 ½ tbsp Rava/Semolina
1 tsp Sugar
1 tsp Ginger and green chilli paste
1 tsp Lemon Juice
Salt to taste
¼ tsp Haldi/Turmeric Powder
¾ Cup Water
1 ½ tsp Eno/Fruit Salt
Coriander leaves (Optional for garnish)

𝐅𝐨𝐫 𝐭𝐞𝐦𝐩𝐞𝐫𝐢𝐧𝐠

1 tsp Mustard Seeds
1 ½ tsp Oil
A Spring of Curry Leaves
1-2 pinches of Hing/Asafetida
2 tbsp Water

𝐌𝐞𝐭𝐡𝐨𝐝

1. Take a pressure cooker/steamer with water and keep it on low flame to boil.
2. Meanwhile in a bowl, taken besan, rava, sugar, ginger and green chilli paste, lemon juice, salt, haldi, water and mix it well. There should be no lumps.
3. Grease the container in which you would steam the dhokla.
4. Add eno to the besan mixture and mix well.
5. Immediately pour it into the greased container and put it the cooker.
6. Close the lid and let it steam for 15 minutes on medium to low flame.
7. After 15 minutes, insert a toothpick or knife to see if the dhokla is done. If the knife comes out clean the dhokla is done.
8. Remove it from the cooked and place it aside while you make the tadka let it cool.
9. In a tadka pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves and hing. After the seeds splutter, switch off the gas and pour 2 tbsp of water into it.
10. Pour the tadka over the dhokla. Let the dhokla absorb the tadka for around 2 minutes and cut it into desired size.
11. Serve with coriander chutney or imli chutney.

Elaichi Chai (Serves 2)


Some have tea only twice a day, whereas some don’t need a reason to drink just like a few of my friends. Chai pe charcha is a favourite term used by many.

𝐈𝐧𝐠𝐫𝐞𝐝𝐢𝐞𝐧𝐭𝐬

1 ½ cup Milk
½ cup Water
3 tsp Tea
3 ½ tsp Sugar (You can add or reduce as per your taste)
2-3 Cardamom (Ground)

𝐌𝐞𝐭𝐡𝐨𝐝

1. Add milk and water to a saucepan.
2. Add sugar, tea and cardamom powder to the same.
3. Let it come to a boil and simmer for 3-4 mins.
4. Strain and serve hot.
