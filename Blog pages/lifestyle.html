Life during a Pandemic



What is a pandemic? We have read about pandemics only in books or heard stories related to it from our grandparents. Plague influenza, small pox, these diseases had affected the lives of our ancestors. Now for the first time we are experiencing a pandemic of such a huge scale. The corona virus which is so tiny, that cannot be seen with our naked eyes has taken the whole world in a storm. Maybe the pandemic might have not spread like wild fire if people had behaved responsibly, mujhe kaise hoga, mujhe toh nahi hoga this attitude of people has hit many. During these times a little fear is better than being too brave.

Humans have always felt that they had conquered everything under the sun. But God had other plans, the virus has shown us that we after so many inventions and discoveries are still so vulnerable and have achieved so little. You and me have taken the life so casually until a few months back, but now we have to go slow. We start feeling scared in a crowd, start doubting if a person just sneezes as many are terrified by this deadly disease. The government is trying it’s best to control the pandemic and trying to make vaccines to eradicate this virus. We all are waiting for that day when this would happen. Narendra Modi had said in his speech that we would go back around 2 decades if this pandemic overpowered us.The pandemic is not only corroding human lives but also affecting the economic growth. Number of people have lost their jobs. The worst state was of the migrant workers. The fear of this disease has led to psychological problems in many. Going to a friend’s house, eating out, going for a movie, stroll in the park or for that matter having road side pani puri is now feeling like a luxury because it now feels out of reach. I like the government concept of my home my family. If each family follows the simple rules, we can keep this virus away from our doorstep. The virus which entered our lives in the beginning of 2020, hope it leaves our lives permanently at the wake of year 2021. In the end I would like to say stay mask, stay safe and stay home.

8:28 ka second last first-class dabba



“Hello” (disturbance in the background due to heavy rain and train siren)

“Nikla Kya?”

“Han pohonch hi raha hun.”

“8:28 na?”

“Han 8:28.”

What is your greatest struggle in life?

Monsoons have their own pros and cons, brings life somewhere and destroys elsewhere, but then that’s how the system works. Conservation of matter.

What are we doing in life these days, we wake up every day to routine life, the same commute, the same people, the same way back home. What have you done different rather than attaching yourself to this monotony. We perceive everything in colour yet live a monochrome life, how different is that?

We name our days, our months, but what have we done new to remember that day by name? how have we lived till now.

If you die today what would be your greatest regret or won’t you have regrets at all?

Ask me and I’ll say the cheapest way to travel to south Bombay is by a local. Always cheapest, never comfortable enough until you get used to it. The first time I probably travelled on train alone would be the first day of college. Always hated them, because I never had the opportunity to be a part of them…

Hey man! How do you get to Mahim?
What would I answer, I had to figure that out myself?

When you are new to something, everything seems tough, but time does all the work for you.

Two years have gone by and ask me now, I can tell you the platform the train would be at Mahim, where you would find TC’s and where you’d find none. How to travel without a ticket, or at which station the TC would get on. Why do I know all this?

Did I have the intent of learning? This was something life taught me, among many other things.

“Churchgate jane vali bara dibbo ki dheemi local, platform no 2 par aa rahi hai!!”
Would always make me rush, in a hurry to get that second last first-class carriage.
Sometimes waiting for a friend, I would sit at a bench between the platform, and watch all those people whirl by.

Always felt my time slowed down. Where were they headed? Who were they? Do we all have the same algorithms or can we alter the codes?

How well can you dance to the symphony of life? Or can you change the symphony to match your moves?

The 20-minute commute from Andheri to Mahim has witnessed the craziest conspiracies, weirdest conversations and the most beautiful moments of life and some good friends.

Rains always messed everything up, but they never failed to create a wonderful feeling inside of you.

The long unending wait before Khar station always messes up schedules, waiting in hopes for the train to move.

A constant group of people, never fail to board the 8:28 ST at platform 2. Always in their same seats, the same people, the same coach always on time.

“Bro…Lecture baithna hai kya?”

“Vapis ghar chale?”

The small incidents make up your memories for life, some distant feeling or a similar smell would live in you forever.

I always wondered how life would be if I were in a different college.

Going back home was tedious.

An increase in frequency of trains to platform no 1 at Borivali would be appreciated. Local to Andheri is quite unpredictable.

“Wakt kaise guzar gaya kabhi pata hi nahi chala, Na jane kuch sapne, sapne hi reh gaye.”

“Bro? kal 8:28 na?”

“Han 8:28.”
